Making vaccine information accessible to all.




TrackMyVaccine is a volunteer-led initiative of creating and managing an open database of the COVID-19 vaccines in Nepal. This citizen campaign tracks the management and distribution of COVID-19 vaccines in the country from multiple reliable sources and informs citizens of their status through an easy-to-navigate comprehensive online dashboard.

In early March 2021, Nepal became one of the first Asian countries to receive COVID-19 vaccines through COVAX, after being one of the first countries in the world to roll-out COVID-19 vaccination back in January 2021. It was believed that this support would accelerate the vaccination efforts of the country. Two months down the line, there is dismay among the general public as the 1.3million senior citizens await their second jab of Covishield vaccine. This has been further fueled by the media stories showing vaccines, which are already in limited stocks, are being accessed by those in power using personal channels. To a large extent, the general public relies on media posts to keep themselves updated about the vaccine landscape (at least on the management and implementation side). Despite significant demand, the government has not made COVID-19 vaccines management data public. As more news on vaccines aid pops in, transparency and accuracy of data on real time vaccine management becomes all the more important. After all, lack of transparency, or worse, misinformation will serve only to erode public trust in government and related stakeholders, which is not ideal.

TrackMyVaccine campaign takes the COVID-19 vaccine information into the public domain. This will not only help an individual to stay informed on the management, but support the Government and relevant agencies in efficient and equitable distribution.
